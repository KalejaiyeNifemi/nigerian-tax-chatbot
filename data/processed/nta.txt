Nigeria Tax Act, 2025
The Nigeria Tax Act, 2025 is the principal substantive tax law of the Federal Republic of Nigeria. It
consolidates, modernises, and replaces fragmented federal tax statutes by providing a single, coherent
framework governing taxable persons, taxable income, transactions, and tax bases in Nigeria.
The Act works alongside the Nigeria Tax Administration Act, 2025 (which deals with procedures,
enforcement, and penalties) and reflects Nigeria’s shift toward a simpler, fairer, and more transparent tax
system
1. Purpose and Policy Direction
The Nigeria Tax Act, 2025 is designed to:
Simplify Nigeria’s tax laws into a unified structure;
Broaden the tax base while reducing distortions;
Improve certainty and predictability for taxpayers;
Support economic growth and investment; and
Align Nigeria’s tax system with modern fiscal and environmental priorities
2. Scope and Application
The Act applies to:
Individuals, companies, partnerships, and other taxable persons;
Income, profits, gains, and specified transactions;
Activities carried on within Nigeria or deemed to have sufficient nexus with Nigeria.
It governs what is taxable, who is taxable, and how tax liability arises, while leaving how tax is administered and
enforced to the Tax Administration Act

4. Introduction of Surcharge
A major innovation of the Act is the formal introduction of surcharge as a distinct tax, separate from penalties
and interest.
The Act introduces fossil fuel surcharge under a dedicated chapter;
Surcharge applies to defined chargeable transactions, not to default or lateness;
It reflects Nigeria’s environmental and energy-transition policy objectives ￼.
5. Exemptions, Incentives, and Reliefs
The Act:
Rationalises tax exemptions and reliefs;
Requires that exemptions be expressly provided by law;
Enhances transparency around tax expenditures.
This reduces discretionary exemptions and strengthens fiscal discipline ￼.

7. Relationship with Other Tax Laws
The Act:
Works in tandem with the Nigeria Tax Administration Act, 2025 for enforcement;
Interfaces with the Nigeria Revenue Service (Establishment) Act, 2025 for institutional administration;
Is coordinated nationally through the Joint Revenue Board of Nigeria framework.
Together, these laws form an integrated tax reform ecosystem.
