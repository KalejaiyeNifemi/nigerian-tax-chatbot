Company Income Tax
Corporate Income Tax (CIT) is governed by the Nigeria Tax Act, 2025, also known as "Company Income Tax"
and administered by the Nigeria Revenue Service (NRS).
The tax is imposed on the income, profits, or gains of a person accruing in or derived from Nigeria.
Chargeable Income
Corporate Income Tax applies to, but is not limited to, income, profits, or gains arising from the following
sources:
Profits or gains from any trade, business, profession, or vocation, for whatever period such activities
are carried on;
Royalties, fees, rents, or interest arising from a right granted for the use, exploitation, or occupation of
any property;
Dividends, premiums, charges, or annuities;
Fees, dues, allowances, or any remuneration for services rendered;
Discounts or rebates;
Income from the disposal of money or money instruments;
Income, profits, or gains from the disposal or lending of securities;
Prizes, winnings, honoraria, grants, awards, laurels, and similar receipts;
Profits or gains from the disposal of property or fixed assets;
Profits or gains from transactions involving digital or virtual assets; and
Any other income, profit, or gain not specifically covered under the preceding categories.

Eligibility to File
Nigerian Company
The profits of a Nigerian company are deemed to accrue in Nigeria wherever the profits arise and
whether or not such profits have been brought into or received in Nigeria. Every taxable person
shall register with the relevant tax authority and obtain a Taxpayer Identification ("Tax ID") for the
purpose of compliance with tax obligations
Non-resident company
The income, profits or gains of a non-resident person accruing in, or derived from Nigeria are
chargeable to tax in accordance with the provisions of this Act. Gains derived by a non-resident
person from disposal of chargeable assets are taxable in Nigeria where the gains relate to –
A trade, business, profession or vocation carried on by the non-resident person in
Nigeria;
Any asset located in Nigeria; or
Any asset deemed to be located in Nigeria under this Act.
A non-resident person that supplies taxable goods or services to any person in Nigeria, or derives
income from Nigeria shall register for tax purposes and obtain a Tax ID : Provided that a non-
resident person who derives only passive income from investment in Nigeria may not be required
to register for tax but shall provide relevant information as may be prescribed by the Service

Electronic Filing of Tax Returns
Taxpayers are required to file their tax returns electronically through the Self Tax Portal of the
Nigeria Revenue Service (NRS).
To file your returns:
Log in to the Self Tax Portal using your registered credentials.
Download the relevant tax return forms applicable to your tax type.
Complete the forms accurately in accordance with applicable tax laws and
guidelines.
Upload the completed forms on the portal to submit your tax returns seamlessly.
Make payment of any assessed tax liability through the approved electronic
payment channels available on the portal.
The Self Tax Portal provides a secure, efficient, and convenient platform for filing returns and
making payments in compliance with statutory obligations.
Manage and Track Your Taxes
Easily track your filed returns and manage your tax obligations through the Self-Tax Portal.
With the portal, you can:
View the status of all submitted tax returns in real time;
Manage your tax obligations across different tax types;
Access and review all your tax accounts in one place; and
View and utilise your Withholding Tax credits seamlessly.

Deadlines and Penalties
Income Tax returns are required to be filed annually in accordance with the Nigeria Tax
Administration Act, 2025 and applicable guidelines issued by the Nigeria Revenue Service (NRS).
Filing Due Dates
Existing Companies:
Returns must be filed within six (6) months after the end of the accounting year
(e.g., 30 June where the accounting year ends on 31 December).
Newly Incorporated Companies:
Returns must be filed within eighteen (18) months from the date of incorporation or six (6) months
after the end of the first accounting period, whichever occurs earlier.
Timely filing and payment of assessed taxes are mandatory. Failure to comply may result in the
imposition of penalties, interest charges, or other enforcement actions in accordance with the
law.
Taxpayers are advised to monitor applicable deadlines and ensure compliance to avoid
sanctions.
Dispute Resolution
Taxpayers who disagree with an Income Tax assessment, demand notice, or computation issued
by the tax Service have the right to object or appeal in accordance with the provisions of the
Nigeria Tax Administration Act, 2025.
Objections and Appeals
An objection or appeal shall be filed within the statutory period of thirty (30) days from the date
of service of the assessment or notice, clearly stating the grounds of objection or appeal and
supported by relevant documentation, in accordance with the provisions of the Nigeria Tax Act,
2025.
